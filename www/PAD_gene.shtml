How do I find mutations in a specific gene?

  1. Click here to go to the Phenotypes and Alleles Query Form. The Phenotypes and Alleles Query Form is located beneath the Search menu, as shown below.

  2. Type kit in the Gene/Marker text box.

  3. Change the drop-down menu to the left of the Gene/Marker text box from "contains" to "=".

  4. Click the Search button.

  5. The search results list all alleles of the Kit gene. The first column supplies the allele symbol and name. Mutation types, such as chemically induced (ENU), knock-in, and spontaneous mutations are described in the Category column. The final column lists genotypes as a combination of Kit alleles on a specific genetic background. Click any allele symbol in the first column of the search results to view greater detail about an allele, genotypes involving this allele, and phenotypes of mice carrying this allele.
