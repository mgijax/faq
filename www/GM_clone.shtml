How do I find genomic or cDNA clones?
Overview
You can use the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form to identify probes or clones for a specific gene or region of the mouse genome. This form may be used to retrieve data about genomic or cDNA clones or primer sequences, and to identify suppliers of genomic or cDNA clones.
Accessing the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form
On most MGI pages, you can access the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form from the Search menu as shown in the image at right.

Example 1. Finding genomic clones for gene Whrn
  1. Change contains to = in the drop-down menu beneath Gene/Marker Symbol/Name.

  2. Type Whrn in the text box.

  3. Select genomic from the Sequence Type drop-down menu.

  4. Click Search.

  1. Click the link to searches/probe.cgi?1030525' onClick="javascript:openNewWindow('searches/probe.cgi?1030525'); return false;"> MGI:2785018.
  1. Click the link to RPCI-23 on the page that follows.
  1. Click the link to RP23-340H1 on the NCBI Clone Registry page.

  2. Order this clone from BACPAC Resources or e-mail BACPACorders@chori.org.

Example 2. Finding cDNA clones for gene Whrn
  1. Follow the steps detailed in Example 1 above, but select cDNA instead of genomic for step 3.

  2. Click Search.

  3. Detail pages for cDNA clones may contain direct links to IMAGE or RIKEN. To order RIKEN clones specifically, visit DNAForm.
