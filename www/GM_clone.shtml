How do I find genomic or cDNA clones?
Overview
You can use the Search Box to find information about genes, markers or accession identifiers in MGI. The Search Box returns a list of genes, markers, or accession IDs that contain your entry.
Accessing the Search Box
On most MGI pages, you can find the Search Box in the upper right corner, as shown in the image.

For this tutorial, open the MGI home page in a new window. Scroll down this page for further instructions.


Example 1. Finding genomic clones for gene Whrn
  1. Type whrn into the Search Box.

  2. Click Quick Search.
  1. Click the link to .
  1. Locate the Molecular Reagents section near the bottom of the page. Click the link to Genomic 3 to view a list of 3 genomic clones containing Whrn.
  1. Click the link to RPCI23 clone RP23-340H1
  1. Click the link to RPCI-23 on the page that follows.
  1. Click on "Distributors" tab to order.

Example 2. Finding cDNA clones for gene Whrn
  1. Follow the steps detailed in Example 1 above, but select cDNA (8) instead of genomic (3) for step 4.
  2. Detail pages for cDNA clones may contain direct links to IMAGE or RIKEN. To order RIKEN clones specifically, visit DNAForm.
