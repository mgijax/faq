How do I find a recombinase-expressing transgene or knock-in that is expressed in a specific tissue?
Overview
You can use the tools listed under "Access Data" to find transgenes that express recombinase.
Find Recombinases-Carrying Alleles
On most MGI pages, you can access the Recombinase (cre) Specificity page from the Search menu as shown in the image at right.

For this tutorial, open the recombinase.shtml' onClick="javascript:openNewWindow('recombinase.shtml'); return false;"> Recombinase (cre) Specificity page in a new window. Scroll down this page for further instructions. The "Find Recombinases-Carrying Alleles" section allows you to search for recombinase expression in a specified tissue or under the control of a given gene.


Example 1. Finding a transgene expressing recombinase in cardiovascular system.
  1. Select cardiovascular system from the Recombinase specificity in selection list.

  2. Click Go.
  1. In the summary page that appears, click the link to "(phenotype data)" below an allele symbol to go to the detail page for more information.
  1. In the Targeted Allele Detail page that appears, go to the Recombinase specificity section and click cardiovascular system for more information.
  1. This returns a page summarizing recombinase expression under the control of Nkx2-5 in the cardiovascular system.
