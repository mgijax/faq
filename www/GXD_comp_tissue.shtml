How do I find genes expressed in one tissue but not another?

  1. Click WIFetch?page=expressionQFexpanded"TARGET = "BLANK">here to open the Gene Expression Data Expanded Query Form in a new window. The Gene Expression Data Expanded Query Form is located beneath the Search menu in the toolbar, as shown below.

  2. Find the Expression section in the center of the form. Notice that this section is divided into four quadrants: two on the left entitled Anatomical Structure(s) and two on the right entitled Developmental Stage(s).

  3. Type the name of a tissue, such as forebrain, into the Anatomical Structures(s) text box in the upper left quadrant. This part of the search will return assays in which expression was detected.

  4. Type the name of a second tissue, such as midbrain, into the Anatomical Structures(s) text box in the lower left quadrant. This part of the search will return assays in which expression was NOT detected.

  5. Click the Search button.

  6. The search results will list genes which are expressed in the forebrain but not in the midbrain. Each gene may be listed more than once, since the structures you have searched for may appear in more than one assay. Click a link in the Results Details column to view assay details.
