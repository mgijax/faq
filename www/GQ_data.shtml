How can I contribute my data to MGI?

Overview
You can submit experimental data to MGI to increase the exposure of your work and to make data available to the scientific community. Submitting experimental data to MGI will increase the exposure of your work because your data will be integrated with many other data and made widely available for database searches, adding to its value and increasing its exposure. Also, by increasing the amount of data in MGI, you can help increase its utility for yourself and others.
Submitting data to MGI
On most MGI pages, click Submit Data in the navigation bar as shown in the image at right.

For this tutorial, open the Submit Data page in a new window or tab.


Submitting allele, phenotype or expression data
  1. For new alleles, mutations, or transgenes, click the link to Allele Nomenclature to retrieve the Mutation Submission Form. Use this form to describe spontaneous, induced, or genetically engineered mutations. MGI staff will help you assign correct nomenclature and provide an MGI accession identifier.

  2. To submit phenotype information for mutations already in MGI, click the link to Phenotype Data to retrieve the Phenotype Data Submission Form.

  3. To submit expression data, click the link to Expression Data to retrieve guidelines for Submitting expression data to the Gene Expression Database (GXD).
