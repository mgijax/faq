How do I find primer sequence for a gene?
Overview
You can use the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form to identify probes or clones for a specific gene or region of the mouse genome. This form may be used to retrieve data about genomic or cDNA clones or primer sequences, and to identify suppliers of genomic or cDNA clones.
Accessing the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form
On most MGI pages, you can access the Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form from the Search menu as shown in the image at right.

For this tutorial, open the WIFetch?page=probeQF' onClick="javascript:openNewWindow('WIFetch?page=probeQF'); return false;"> Molecular Probes and Clones Query Form in a new window.


Example 1. Finding primer sequence for gene Myo6
  1. Type myo6 in the text box beneath Gene/Marker Symbol/Name.

  2. Select primer from the Sequence Type drop-down menu.

  3. Click Search.

  1. Click the link to searches/probe.cgi?35075' onClick="javascript:openNewWindow('searches/probe.cgi?35075'); return false;"> MGI:6655 in the Nucleotide Probe/Clone AccID column to view details about primer sequences, alleles, relative fragment sizes and strains.
