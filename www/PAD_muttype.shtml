How do I find all of one kind of allele (e.g. cre transgenes, LoxP/Frt-flanked alleles, etc.)?

  1. Click here to go to the Phenotypes and Alleles Query Form. The Phenotypes and Alleles Query Form is located beneath the Search menu, as shown below.

  2. In the Categories section in the lower half of the page, check the box next to transgenic (Cre/Flp).

  3. In the Sorting and output format section at the bottom of the page, check the box next to no limit to clear the default limit of 500 on the number of results returned.

  4. Click the Search button.

  5. Click any allele symbol in the first column of the search results to view greater detail about an allele.

  6. Use your browser's back button to return to the Phenotypes and Alleles Query form. Uncheck the box next to transgenic (Cre/Flp), and check the box next to targeted (Floxed/Frt).

  7. Click the Search button.

  8. Click any allele symbol in the first column of the search results to view detail about an allele.
