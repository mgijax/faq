How do I find expression data for a whole tissue during development?
Overview

You can use The Mouse Anatomical Dictionary Browser to find anatomical terms of most interest to you, either by browsing the hierarchy of anatomical structures or by searching for specific terms. You can then use the links provided to find the expression results associated with those terms.

Accessing the Mouse Anatomical Dictionary Browser
On most MGI pages, you can access the Mouse Anatomical Dictionary Browser from the Search menu as shown in the image at right.

Example 1. Finding expression results for the heart in Theiler stage 17

  1. Type heart into the Query text box.

  2. Select TS 17 (10.0-11.25 dpc) (Theiler Stage 17, 10.0-11.25 days post conception. Browse the anatomical dictionary below for term details) from the Developmental Stage(s) box.

    Hold the Shift or Ctrl key to select multiple stages simultaneously.

  3. Click the Search button.

  1. Click on heart from the results to see heart-associated expression results.

  1. Click on show all to see all expression results annotated to the heart and its substructures.

  1. The Results table indicates the genes for which data is returned, assays (independent experiments with references), assay results and images in the tabs along the top.

  2. Sort data by clicking on the column headers and/or export using the buttons above the table.

  3. Click the data hyperlink in the Result Details column to view details from assays that examined expression in the heart in Theiler stage 17.

  1. The Images tab allows you to quickly view expression images for the selected genes and tissues.

    Click on Result Details to see assay details, annotations, and associated reference for each image.
