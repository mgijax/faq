How do I use attributes such as genome location, disease association, phenotype, or function to find specific genes?

  1. Click here to open the Genes and Markers Query Form in a new window. The Genes and Markers Query Form is located beneath the Search menu, as shown below.

  2. In the Gene/Marker section of the page, find the Type drop-down menu and select Gene.

  3. To identify genes within a specific genomic location, find the Map Position section of the form. As an example, select 7 from the Chromosome(s) list.

  4. In the text box beneath Genome Coordinates, type 130 and 140.

  5. Change the pull-down menu near the Genome Coordinates text box from bp to Mbp.

  6. To limit this search to genes associated with a specific function, find the Gene Ontology classifications section in the lower half of the form.

  7. In the text box beneath Gene Ontology (GO) Classifications, type bone mineralization.

  8. To further limit this search to genes associated with specific phenotypes or diseases, find the Mouse phenotypes & mouse models of human disease section in the lower half of the form.

  9. In the text box beneath Phenotype/Human Disease, type craniofacial.

  10. Click the Search button. The search results will list all genes on chromosome 7 between 130 and 140 Mbp that are associated with bone mineralization and a craniofacial phenotype.
