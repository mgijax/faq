How do I create a genome map from a genomic interval?

  1. Click homepages/index.shtml"TARGET = "BLANK">here to open the MGI home page in a new window. Select Mouse GBrowse from the Search menu in the toolbar, as shown below.

  2. Mouse GBrowse represents an entire chromosome as a line with demarcations in mega base pairs (M). In the image below, the Overview displays all of chromosome 7. The red line located between 130M and 140M indicates the region of chromosome 7 displayed in the Details section. The line in the Details section displays chromosome 7 from 136,732 to 136,969 kilobase pairs (k).

  3. Find the Landmark or Region text box, and notice that the region displayed in detail is chromosome 7 from 136,732,000 to 136,969,000 base pairs, written as 7:136732000..136969000. To view a different interval on chromosome 7, change the numbers representing base pairs. For example, change the text in the Landmark or Region text box to 7:101000000..103000000 to view chromosome 7 from 101M to 103M.

  4. Click the Search button at the right of the text box.

  5. Scroll down the page to the Tracks section.

  6. The Tracks section permits display of alleles, genes, phenotypes associated with alleles, QTL, SNPs and much more. To display some of these features on the map, check the boxes next to the following:
    • Alleles
      • Alleles_targeted
    • Genes
      • VEGA transcripts
    • Phenotypes
      • Behavior_neurological
      • Digestive_alimentary
      • Growth_size
      • Homeostasis_metabolism
      • Immune_system
      • QTL

  7. Click the Update Image button at the lower right of the Tracks section.
