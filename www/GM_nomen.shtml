How do I find or obtain approved nomenclature for a gene, allele or strain?


Overview
You can use the Mouse Nomenclature Home Page to identify correct nomenclature for genes, markers, alleles, strains, mutations, and chromosome aberrations. You can also submit a proposed locus symbol, submit mutant allele information, register a new mouse strain name, or apply for a laboratory registration code from this page.
Accessing the Mouse Nomenclature Home Page

Rules and Guidelines
Refer to the Quick Guides for gene, allele and mutation nomenclature. More detailed information may be found in the full guides. As an example, allele symbol Egfrtm1Cwr refers to the first targeted mutation (tm1) of the Egfr gene produced by Case Western Reserve University (Cwr).
Searches
Search or apply for a lab code by clicking the appropriate link to the Institute for Laboratory Animal Research (ILAR).
Submissions/Registrations
Submit data by clicking the links to Submit a Proposed Locus Symbol or Mutant Allele Information, or Register a new Mouse Strain Name.
