Overview
You can use Quick Search to find genes by function, disease, phenotype, identifier or other types of data in MGI. Quick Search returns a list of genome features, terms, or other search results that match your entry.
Accessing Quick Search
On most MGI pages, you can find Quick Search in the upper right corner.

For this tutorial, open the homepages/index.shtml' onClick="javascript:openNewWindow('homepages/index.shtml'); return false;"> MGI home page in a new window and locate Quick Search at the top center of the page. Scroll down this page for further instructions.


Example 1. Finding data associated with a list of genes or identifiers
  1. Type 17222948, P32921, Pax3 into the text box.

  2. Click Quick Search.
  1. The Quick Search Results rank genome features, vocabulary terms, and other results matching your entries by relevance.

  2. Click Get More Data for genome features 1 through 5 to retrieve associated data such as gene function, phenotype, allele or disease data.
  1. Select the radio button next to Human Disease (OMIM) to retrieve human diseases associated with mouse genes Wars and Pax3, Cre transgenes Tg(Pax3-cre)1Joe and Tg(Pax3-cre)2Joe, and gene trap Gt(ROSA)26Sor.

  2. Click Search Again.

Example 2. Finding data associated with vitamin D binding
  1. Type vitamin D binding into the text box.

  2. Click Quick Search.
  1. The Quick Search Results rank genome features, vocabulary terms, and other results containing vitamin D binding by relevance to this phrase, or any individual word in this phrase.

    Click the link to and 10 more... in the Best Match column. This lists related matches (i.e. protein domains, diseases) associated with gene Gc.
